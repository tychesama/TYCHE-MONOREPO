Every project has a beginning, and for me, this website started as something simple: a personal portfolio. Over time, it gradually evolved into something more meaningful—a small corner of the internet that represents my work, interests, and progress as a developer.

## The Original Domain

Before this site became **joemidpan.com**, it originally lived under a different domain: **tyche01.fun**.

At the time, it worked fine as a place to experiment and deploy projects. However, the more I thought about it, the more it felt a bit too casual and unprofessional for something that might represent me publicly.

Eventually, I decided to switch to a domain that used my **own name instead**. It felt simpler, more direct, and more appropriate for a personal website.

## Starting With a Portfolio

The first version of the website was just a **portfolio**.

The goal was straightforward: create a place where I could display projects and show what I had been learning. It wasn’t meant to be complicated or feature-heavy—just a clean place to present my work.

At the time, I simply wanted something that could represent my skills and progress.

## Designing Without Fancy Tools

Interestingly, the design process didn’t involve modern design tools.

I didn’t use Figma or any advanced prototyping software. Instead, most ideas were sketched using:

- **Pen and paper**
- **Rough drawings**
- **Even simple sketches in Paint**

It wasn’t a formal design process, but it worked. Sometimes simple sketches are enough to organize thoughts and turn ideas into something real.

## The Motivation Behind It

Part of the motivation for creating the site came from **suggestions from friends during my internship**.

They recommended building a personal site as a way to showcase work and create a stronger online presence. At the same time, I was already curious about how websites are built and deployed, so the idea quickly became interesting.

It turned into a project that combined **curiosity, learning, and a bit of encouragement from others**.

## My First Deployed Website

This project also holds a small milestone for me.

It was the **first website I ever deployed with my own domain**. Seeing something you built accessible on the internet under your own name is a surprisingly satisfying moment.

It makes the project feel more real.

## A Place for Past and Future Work

Although it started as a portfolio, the plan was always for the site to become more than that.

Over time, I wanted it to become a **compilation of things related to me**, including:

- Projects
- Experiments
- Articles or notes
- Personal ideas

In a way, it’s meant to be both a **record of past work** and a **place for future projects**.

## A Monorepo of Ideas

From a development perspective, the site is organized as a **monorepo**.

This structure allows multiple parts of the website and its projects to exist within a single repository. What began as a small portfolio slowly expanded into something that could support multiple projects without needing to create separate repositories for everything.

It keeps things organized while allowing the site to grow.

## Growing Beyond a Portfolio

Originally, the website was meant to be **only a portfolio**.

But as I continued building and experimenting, new ideas started appearing. Small features turned into projects, and projects slowly became part of the website itself.

What began as a simple showcase gradually evolved into a **collection of things I’ve built or explored**.

## A Reflection of Myself

In the end, the design philosophy of the site is simple.

The website tries to highlight a few qualities that I value:

- **Straightforward design**
- **Simplicity**
- **Functionality over unnecessary complexity**

It isn’t meant to be overly flashy or complicated. Instead, the focus is on clarity and usefulness.

## Looking Ahead

This site will probably continue to evolve.

New projects, experiments, and ideas will likely find their way here over time. What matters most is that it remains a place where I can build, experiment, and document the things I create.

For now, **joemidpan.com** stands as the beginning of that journey.
