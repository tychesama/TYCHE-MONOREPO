Every year tends to end faster than expected. When I looked back at the previous one, I realized that a lot had happened—some things good, some things difficult, and many things that quietly shaped how I think now. Because of that, this year feels like a moment to commit more intentionally to growth, especially in areas that matter to me.

This is less about making perfect resolutions and more about setting clear directions for myself.

## Involving Myself More in the Tech Community

One thing I want to do more often is **participate in talks, workshops, and tech-related events**. Learning on my own is valuable, but being around other people in the field exposes me to different ideas, experiences, and ways of thinking.

Even just attending these events can make a difference. Conversations, questions, and listening to experienced developers often teach things that documentation alone cannot.

## Building Real Projects

I also want to focus on creating **projects that reflect the work of a full-stack developer**.

At the moment, I’m not entirely sure whether I want to specialize as a **front-end developer** or a **back-end developer**. Instead of forcing a decision early, the plan is simple: build projects that include both.

By doing this, I can naturally discover which side I enjoy more while still developing a well-rounded skill set.

## The Daily Commit Challenge

Consistency is something I want to improve.

One idea I’ve set for myself is a **challenge to commit code at least once a day**. It doesn’t have to be something massive every time—sometimes it could be a small fix, a minor improvement, or even documentation.

The goal is not perfection but **building the habit of progress**.

## Using AI More Thoughtfully

Tools powered by AI have become incredibly helpful for developers. However, I’ve realized that relying on them too heavily can sometimes prevent deeper understanding.

This year, I want to **reduce my dependence on AI tools like ChatGPT**, or at least use them more intentionally.

Instead of immediately asking for answers, I want to try:

- Solving problems myself first
- Researching documentation
- Understanding the reasoning behind solutions

AI should assist thinking, not replace it.

## Finding a Job by the End of the Year

Another clear goal is to **secure a job before the year ends**.

This means improving my portfolio, strengthening my technical skills, and continuously building projects that demonstrate what I can actually do. The journey may take time, but having a target gives the effort direction.

## Mastering One or Two Languages

This goal is slightly playful, but still meaningful.

I want to **master one or two languages**.

And by languages, I mean either:

- **Programming languages**, or  
- **Spoken languages**

Ideally both.

Being fluent in code allows you to build things. Being fluent in human languages allows you to connect with people. Both feel valuable in their own way.

## Looking Back at Last Year

The previous year was surprisingly profound.

There were moments of learning, frustration, small victories, and quiet progress that didn’t seem important at the time but now feel meaningful. Experiences like these shape how you approach the next stage of your life.

Sometimes growth isn't loud. It happens slowly, almost invisibly, until you suddenly realize you've changed.

## Moving Forward

This year isn't about dramatic transformations. Instead, it's about **steady improvement and intentional effort**.

If I can attend more events, build meaningful projects, stay consistent with commits, think more independently, and move closer to a career in tech, then the year will have been worthwhile.

Progress doesn't always need to be huge. Sometimes it just needs to be consistent.
